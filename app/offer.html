What makes a good offer? Four things.

  1. A real problem. Something that costs your buyer money, time, or sleep. Not a nice-to-have.
  2. What you'd do about it. And why your way beats the usual way.
  3. Pricing that favours the buyer. Cheaper than the alternative, or tied to results.
  4. A promise that removes their risk. Pay after the work is done, pay per result, or get a full refund if we miss. Each promise comes with one fair condition so nobody gets burned.

Real offers that got replies

These lines are word-for-word from real cold emails we sent that got interested replies. Names are swapped out for privacy. Notice how each one makes a promise, removes risk, or offers something small and free.

"If it fits, we can build or run it for you guaranteeing 30 qualified leads in 90 days or we refund you."

A promise with a full refund. 30+ interested replies.

"You only pay after we've built it, so zero upfront amount."

Pay after the work is done. 12 interested replies.

"If it lands, we can run it for you on a pay-per-lead basis, so you only pay for the leads we deliver."

Pay per result. Several booked calls came from this.

"I'd happily put together a mood board, a render, and a sourcing snapshot, and have it back within 48 hours, no charge or commitment."

A free sample, no strings. 38 interested replies, our best line.

"We've put together a quick breakdown of the exact campaigns we'd run to get {{company}} 30 qualified leads a month."

A free worked example for that exact company. 7 interested replies.
