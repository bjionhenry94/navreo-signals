New here? 60-second glossary
Out of office (OOO). An automatic "I'm away" reply. Not a real answer either way. A high OOO share can hide a campaign that's actually fine.
Sends per positive. How many emails go out before one lands an interested reply. Lower is better. 1,500 is the book's break-even from past campaigns. Worse than that and an offer has never paid for its list.bar 1,500
Bounce rate. The share of emails that failed to deliver. Above 2%, inbox providers start treating the sender as suspicious. That's why it's the bar.bar 2%
Speed to lead. How long it takes a human to reply once someone shows interest. Healthy is hours, not days.
Worked. A lead's sending capacity is ~2 emails; the ring shows how much of the whole list's capacity is used.
Traffic split. What share of sends each version of an email gets. A broken split can silently stop a version sending at all.
Warm lead. Someone who replied positively: interested, wants more info, or asked to meet.
Dismissed (Setter). Bjion saw the reply in the Setter and chose not to pursue it (unqualified, wrong fit, whatever the reason). Dismissed replies count as handled, never as waiting.
